The First Workshop on Sovereign AI for Collaborative and Pluralistic AI Ecosystems

Examining how AI systems that claim to serve local law, language, culture, and the public interest can remain accountable while depending on global models, data, compute, and institutions.

i.

Overview

As AI systems become increasingly central to how societies make decisions, questions of sovereignty, autonomy, and pluralism become urgent. This workshop convenes researchers, practitioners, and policymakers to examine how AI ecosystems can be designed to preserve individual and collective agency, support diverse values, and enable collaborative governance rather than centralized control.

ii.

Topics of Interest

We invite contributions across five broad themes, each welcoming technical, design, and policy perspectives; evaluation, benchmarking, and human-centered evidence cut across all of them.

01

Safety, Alignment, and Management of Agentic and Physical AI

Autonomy, embodiment, multimodality, safety evaluation, and meaningful human control.

Example question: How do we ensure that increasingly autonomous and embodied AI systems remain safe, aligned, and under meaningful human control?

02

Human Oversight, Agency, and Trustworthy AI Interaction

Delegation, trust calibration, usable privacy and transparency, intervention, and recovery.

Example question: How can people effectively understand, oversee, and intervene in AI systems without losing their own agency?

03

Open Models, Data Governance, and Resilient AI Deployment

Open-weight and foundation models, local adaptation, data access and quality, evaluation benchmarks, compute/API dependencies, portability, and continuity of service.

Example question: How can we build open and adaptable AI systems that remain reliable without depending entirely on a few models, datasets, or service providers?

04

Language, Culture, and Civic AI for the Public Interest

Local-language and culturally grounded AI, cultural representation, participatory design, public services, and media literacy.

Example question: How can people from different technical, cultural, and professional backgrounds help build AI that better serves diverse languages, communities, and public needs?

05

Democratic Resilience and Multi-Stakeholder AI Governance

Public accountability, agile regulatory design, institutional coordination, deliberation, and co-governance.

Example question: How can governments, researchers, industry, and the public meaningfully participate in governing AI and holding it accountable?

The list above is illustrative, not exhaustive. We welcome any work relevant to sovereign, collaborative, and pluralistic AI.

iv.

Call for Papers

Sovereign AI invites researchers and practitioners to treat sovereign AI as a human-AI complementarity, interaction, and governance problem: when AI systems claim to serve local law, language, culture, and the public interest while depending on global models, data, compute, and institutions, how should they be built, used, evaluated, and governed?

Building on the five themes above, we welcome work from:

AI / ML & Industry

Safety, alignment, control, open models, data governance, evaluation, and resilient deployment.

HCI, Design & Social Computing

Oversight, agency, trust calibration, usable privacy, participatory design, and civic AI.

Law, Policy & Civil Society

Democratic resilience, public-interest AI, agile governance, and multi-stakeholder coordination.

Submissions: We welcome full papers, work-in-progress papers, position papers, surveys, system demonstrations, and deployment case studies that surface real dilemmas and failures.

Full papers may be up to 6000 words (excluding references), while all other submission types should be 2-4 pages (excluding references) using the ACM conference format.

Accepted submissions will be presented as talks, posters, or interactive demonstrations and will contribute to the workshop’s cross-disciplinary discussions and collaborative outcomes.

See General Submission Instructions for the official ACM templates and formatting policies.
